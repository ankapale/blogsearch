<div id="ban3_av"></div><h2 style="text-align: justify;">Introduction</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">There are 3 fundamental changes driving penetration ofÂ data science industry:</p>

<li style="text-align: justify;">Computing power has become cheap</li>
<li style="text-align: justify;">Numerous tools are available for an analyst to expedite his / her work. Compared to 3 years back, there are tools for faster data collection and cleaning, evolving algorithms and visualization tools.</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify;">These forces have also changed the process flow for an analyst. Effective data visualization is more critical component of data science process flow than ever before. The impact can be felt in following areas clearly:</p>

<h2>What are the common methods of visualization?</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Letâs look at someÂ common methods of visualization which help us to understand distribution, trend, relationship, comparison and composition about the data values. One of the important question, every data scientist facesÂ is to decide, &#8216;<strong>Which visualization method is more effective?</strong>&#8216;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">While discussing the same problem with my <a href="http://discuss.analyticsvidhya.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">community</a>, I got significant upvotes on the method of first focusing on the â<strong>Type of messageÂ or information</strong>â you want to conveyÂ and then select the appropriate visualization method.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Here is a really cool cheat sheet on selecting right visualization methods.Â I found this in HarvardÂ CS-109 extension program (online resource).</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection.jpeg><img class="aligncenter wp-image-15987 size-full" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection.jpeg" alt="Chart_Selection" width="960" height="719" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection.jpeg 960w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection-300x225.jpeg 300w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection-83x63.jpeg 83w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Chart_Selection-850x637.jpeg 850w" sizes="(max-width: 960px) 100vw, 960px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Here you can notice, how flawlesslyÂ they have divided the chart messages in four categories namely <strong>Distribution</strong>, <strong>Comparison</strong>, <strong>Relationship</strong> and <strong>Composition. </strong>Then, they haveÂ classified various visualization methods in these 4 categories.</p>
<p>Letâs look at these categoriesÂ individually and discuss the most common and effective methods in detail.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>1. Distribution</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It is commonlyÂ used at the initial stage of data exploration i.e. when we get started with understanding the variable. Variables are of two types: <em>Continuous</em> and <em>Categorical</em>. For continuous variable, we look at the centre, spread, outlier. For categorical variable we look at frequency table. Visualization typesÂ used to representÂ these are:-</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>a) Histogram: </strong>It isÂ used for showing the distribution of continuous variables. One of the catch with histogram is &#8216;number of bins&#8217;. Letâs understand it in detail using example below:-</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Histogram.png><img class="aligncenter wp-image-15988 size-full" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Histogram.png" alt="Histogram" width="644" height="209" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Histogram.png 644w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Histogram-300x97.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 644px) 100vw, 644px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Both histograms are showing different distribution of a given set of data which representsÂ age distributionÂ usingÂ Count of Passengers vs Age. Look at the histogram at right .We can infer thatÂ there are more infants inÂ age group of 0-4 years compared to age group of 4-16 years. However, if you try to make this inference from left graph, I&#8217;m sure you would fail to do so. Hence, we should be very careful while selecting number of bins.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>b) Box-Plot: </strong>It is used to display full range of variation from min to max and useful to identify outlier values. It shows Min, Q1, Median, Q3 and Max. Any value outside the lower and upper inferences is considered as an Outlier. Formula for calculating lower and Upper inferences are:-</p>
<p>Upper Inference = Q3 + 1.5 * (Q3-Q1), (Q3-Q1) (also known as IQR)</p>
<p>Lower Inference = Q1 &#8211; 1.5 * (Q3-Q1)</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Box_Plot.png><img class=" size-full wp-image-15989 aligncenter" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Box_Plot.png" alt="Box_Plot" width="291" height="197" /></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">We can also visualizeÂ distribution between Â two continuous variables or one categorical and one continuous variable using scatter plot or multiple box plots by different categories of categorical variables respectively.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>2 ) Comparison</h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It is used to compare values across different categories and over time (trend). Common charts to represent these information are Bar and Line chart. Please note:Â When we compare values across different categories we should go with Bar chart. If it is over quantitative variable, we should go with line chart.</p>

<h3>3) Relationship</h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It is widely used to understand the correlation between two or more continuous variables. Most common method to visualize this information is Scatter Plot. It clearly shows the relationship between two variables. It usually draws a line of fit that best represent the relationship between data points.Â The line necessarily need not connect with data points.</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/scatter-plot.gif><img class=" size-full wp-image-15997 aligncenter" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/scatter-plot.gif" alt="scatter-plot" width="198" height="169" /></a></p>
<p>We can also add third variable in scatter plot by using size of the points (known as Bubble Chart) or colors(as shown below).</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BubbleChart.png><img class=" size-medium wp-image-15999 aligncenter" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BubbleChart-300x200.png" alt="BubbleChart" width="300" height="200" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BubbleChart-300x200.png 300w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/BubbleChart.png 420w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">One of the problem with scatter plot is that it may get crowded ifÂ we have thousands or millions of data points. In this case,we can performÂ alpha blending, which makes each point slightly transparent. So regions appear darker that have more point plotted on them.</p>

<h2>Advance Visualization Methods</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Till here, we looked at most common methods used to visualize information. Let&#8217;s look at someÂ advanceÂ methods of visualization. These methods extend the power of storytelling using visualization methods.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>a) Heat Map</strong>: It uses colors to represent numbers in a spreadsheet or in any other visualization methods like Scatter, Geo-spatial, Area Chart. You can set different color gradient for the lowest, highest and mid-range values, with a corresponding transition (or gradient) between these extremes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It represents one more variable to existing visualization method and adds additional information about data values.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat_Map.png><img class="aligncenter wp-image-16008 " src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat_Map.png" alt="Heat_Map" width="403" height="276" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat_Map.png 900w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat_Map-300x205.png 300w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat_Map-850x582.png 850w" sizes="(max-width: 403px) 100vw, 403px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong style="line-height: 1.5;">b) GeoSpatial charts</strong><span style="line-height: 1.5;">: Data scientists started plotting variables onÂ geographical address toÂ help organisations in makingÂ their strategies differently for different cluster based on the spread of data. Here we can also use color index or size as a metric to represent more variables. It is similar to scatter plot. The only difference here is we are plotting data points on map.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">AdvantagesÂ of using Geo-Spatial Visualization are:</p>
<ul>
<li>We can easily understand the distribution of organizations presence across the map.</li>
<li style="text-align: justify;">Easy to represent high number of locations compare to tabular or graphical representation</li>
<li>More intuitive decision-making for the business owner</li>
</ul>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Geo.png><img class="aligncenter wp-image-16005 " src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Geo-1024x373.png" alt="Geo" width="714" height="260" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Geo.png 1024w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Geo-300x109.png 300w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Geo-850x310.png 850w" sizes="(max-width: 714px) 100vw, 714px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Above, you can see that how easily we are able to present information on map compare to tabular and Bar Charts.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>c) Grid:Â </strong>It is used in 2D tabular format. In this method, we use two metrics horizontally and vertically. Then, plot a grid against each category of both metrics. After that, use colors to represent the data.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s understand it using an example:</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Grid.png><img class="aligncenter wp-image-16012 size-full" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Grid.png" alt="Grid" width="547" height="106" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Grid.png 547w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Grid-300x58.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 547px) 100vw, 547px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">In above grid, you can represent the skill of a professional across various tools and techniques. In here, Green color represents the expert, Amber to intermediate and Red as beginner. Here we have efficiently represent this information without much hassle.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>d) WordCloud: </strong>A word cloud is a method to represent text data. It is also known as text or tag cloud. It is a graphical representation of frequently used words in a collectionÂ of text files. The height and font style of each word in this pictureÂ is an indication of frequency of occurrence of the word in the entire text data. Such diagrams are very useful in working onÂ text analytics.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">It is just like any infographic. It makes an impact, easy to understand and can be shared easily. Make a note,Â before using word cloud, only focus on frequency of word and notÂ on importance of variable.</p>
<p><a rel="lightbox" href=https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Word_Coud_1.png><img class=" size-medium wp-image-16025 aligncenter" src="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Word_Coud_1-300x191.png" alt="Word_Coud_1" width="300" height="191" srcset="https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Word_Coud_1-300x191.png 300w, https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Word_Coud_1.png 359w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">There is more advance visualization techniques like Venn-diagram, Network Map, Radar Chart and other custom visualization methods to represent data and generate more meaningful insight from them.</p>
