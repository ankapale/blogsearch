OUR INSPIRATION</h3><p name="9a93" id="9a93" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithSingleQuote graf-after--h3">‘<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Convenience</strong>’ is closely associated with the word ‘<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Conversation</strong>’ which we human beings are so good at. Conversation and ability to have a diverse language set to communicate is one of those traits that distinguished the humans from the rest of the animal world. This was the inspiration which also drove <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Maker’s lab</strong> at <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Tech Mahindra </strong>towards building our own conversational framework.</p><p name="e724" id="e724" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The idea of chat bots has been around since long time. Taking a leap from the first dial tone landlines which allowed us to ring dial a number to get our account information was the earliest most primitive format of a chatbot. Being primitive, it was also not cognitive and worked on simple command sets (or number dialling sets) to provide information. As software took shape, IVR and other techniques allowed enterprises to serve customers across the world. Slightly better than a ring dial(<em class="markup--em markup--p-em">some might say complicated as well</em>) were IVR decision trees which were the first stage intelligent chatbots with an ability to define a decision tree based on what the user dialled at the other end of the line.</p><p name="c104" id="c104" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">As mobility progressed and mobile phones became omni-present, the IVRs were replaced by Visual IVRs and myriads of applications on phones took away the need for humans to call up call centres and engage with primitive chatbots. We are at the dusk of this age where enterprises reached a stage where apps galore and an enterprise finds it difficult to manage the myriads of them for customers and employees alike. This brings us to current state when we are seeing a shift towards natural language UI and the need for conversation to replace these many apps using chatbots. Before we go ahead and explain as to how we built our own proprietary framework, <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Entellio</strong> , it is worthwhile to get some of the basics out of the way.</p><h3 name="f832" id="f832" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">3. WHAT ARE CHATBOTS ?</h3><p name="f1b1" id="f1b1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Chatbots are also called Conversational Agents or Dialog Systems. Microsoft is making big bets on chatbots, and so are companies like Facebook (M), Apple (Siri), Google, WeChat, and Slack. There is a new wave of startups trying to change how consumers interact with services by building consumer apps like Operator or x.ai, bot platforms like Chatfuel, and bot libraries like Howdy’s Botkit. Microsoft recently released their own bot developer framework.</p><p name="da5b" id="da5b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Many companies are hoping to develop bots to have natural conversations indistinguishable from human ones, and many are claiming to be using NLP and Deep Learning techniques to make this possible. But with all the hype around AI it’s sometimes difficult to tell fact from fiction.</p><h3 name="da22" id="da22" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">4. TAXONOMY OF CHATBOT MODELS</h3><p name="7ae4" id="7ae4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Taxonomy of chatbot models have been well explained in the past. I am picking up an article e published by WildML to explain the same and what taxonomic models did we use to build our own internal framework</p><p name="0834" id="0834" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Reference for more reading</strong> : <a href="http://www.wildml.com/2016/04/deep-learning-for-chatbots-part-1-introduction/" data-href="http://www.wildml.com/2016/04/deep-learning-for-chatbots-part-1-introduction/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">http://www.wildml.com/2016/04/deep-learning-for-chatbots-part-1-introduction/</a></p><p name="e8df" id="e8df" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">A.</strong> <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Retrieval-Based vs. Generative Models</strong></p><p name="901c" id="901c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Retrieval-based models (easier)</strong> use a repository of predefined responses and some kind of heuristic to pick an appropriate response based on the input and context. The heuristic could be as simple as a rule-based expression match, or as complex as an ensemble of Machine Learning classifiers. These systems don’t generate any new text; they just pick a response from a fixed set.</p><p name="6018" id="6018" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Generative models (harder)</strong> don’t rely on pre-defined responses. They generate new responses from scratch. Generative models are typically based on Machine Translation techniques, but instead of translating from one language to another, we “translate” from an input to an output (response).</p><p name="f33e" id="f33e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">B.</strong> <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Long vs. Short Conversations</strong></p><p name="ecd2" id="ecd2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The longer the conversation are, the more difficult it is to automate them. On one side of the spectrum are Short-Text Conversations (easier) where the goal is to create a single response to a single input. For example, you may receive a specific question from a user and reply with an appropriate answer. Then there are long conversations (harder) where you go through multiple turns and need to keep track of what has been said. Customer support conversations are typically long conversational threads with multiple questions.</p><p name="d232" id="d232" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">C.</strong> <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Open Domain vs. Closed Domain</strong></p><p name="03b2" id="03b2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In an open domain (harder) setting the user can take the conversation anywhere. There isn’t necessarily have a well-defined goal or intention. Conversations on social media sites like Twitter and Reddit are typically open domain — they can go into all kinds of directions. The infinite number of topics and the fact that a certain amount of world knowledge is required to create reasonable responses makes this a hard problem.</p><p name="c4a5" id="c4a5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In a closed domain (easier) setting the space of possible inputs and outputs is somewhat limited because the system is trying to achieve a very specific goal. Technical Customer Support or Shopping Assistants are examples of closed domain problems. These systems don’t need to be able to talk about politics; they just need to fulfill their specific task as efficiently as possible. Sure, users can still take the conversation anywhere they want, but the system isn’t required to handle all these cases — and the users don’t expect it to.</p><p name="4acb" id="4acb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">So while constructing “Entellio , we chose the combination of the following</p><p name="2fd1" id="2fd1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">a) Retrieval based taxonomy based on word vector semantics (an unsupervised technique of word vector model)</p><p name="2d54" id="2d54" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">b) A hybrid of both long and short conversation in the mix</p><p name="cc56" id="cc56" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">c) Closed domain : Our major focus is to ensure conversations were centred around the enterprise context , so for e.g. let’s take an example of a telecom like a Vodafone serving it’s customers in India. Vodafone provides a plan called “red” which is available in Rs. 699. Now “red” is a colour but is a plan as well, so when a customer is talking to the Vodafone chatbot , the premise of red should not be an erstwhile colour but a plan, an example of semantic context mapping within a specific enterprise</p><h3 name="3657" id="3657" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">5. DEEP DIVE INTO ENTELLIO</h3><p name="0169" id="0169" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">As mentioned above, Entellio was constructed with the following three taxonomic designs in mind</p><p name="4309" id="4309" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">a) Retrieval based</p><p name="af96" id="af96" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">b) Long Short Hybrid conversations</p><p name="3982" id="3982" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">c) Closed Domain</p><p name="8d43" id="8d43" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Entellio also uses the following architectural design techniques</p><p name="e689" id="e689" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">a) Extreme <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Simplicity </strong>to kick start conversations: Conversations should start instantaneously within the framework. It should be as simple as dropping a CSV /EXCEL file of a given FAQ (which enterprises have as standard operating procedures) to enable users to have semantically meaningful conversations.</p><p name="b3be" id="b3be" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">b) <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">YAGNI</strong> (You ain’t gonna need it principle) : If a certain component has no value , it’s not needed and need not be forced into design of the framework.</p><p name="5d37" id="5d37" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">c) <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Separation of concerns</strong>: A very well demarcated separation of concerns between the front end, middle ware (machine learning server) and database is maintained to ensure none of the components get intertwined in glue like pattern. Coupling is avoided and cohesion is maintained between various modules. This cohesion is maintained by a REST based services model.</p><p name="cffd" id="cffd" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A deep dive into Entellio would not be complete unless we can visually explain what we mean by the same, so I would try and explain the entire framework with visual sections explaining technology wherever needed for better understanding</p><h3 name="ac69" id="ac69" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">6. Word2vec takes as its input a large corpus of text and produces a vector space, typically of several hundred dimensions, with each unique word in the corpus being assigned a corresponding vector in the space. Word vectors are positioned in the vector space such that words that share common contexts in the corpus are located in close proximity to one another in the space.</p><p name="665c" id="665c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The conceptualization that happens within the code happens as following. The vectors also help us play with language words because only a numerical value can help us perform mathematical functions on it.</p><p name="e967" id="e967" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A typical word vector of a corpuslooks like the following</p><p name="d500" id="d500" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">[</p><p name="9463" id="9463" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">[0.01, -.02, 0.4, 0l67……………………………………….0.45],</p><p name="3f74" id="3f74" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">[0.11, -.03, 0.4, 0l67……………………………………….0.45],</p><p name="03a9" id="03a9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">[0.01, -.02, 0.4, 0l67……………………………………….0.45],</p><p name="9a97" id="9a97" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">.</p><p name="abaa" id="abaa" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">.</p><p name="a935" id="a935" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">.</p><p name="9768" id="9768" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">[0.01, -.02, 0.4, 0l67……………………………………….0.45],</p><p name="6dd6" id="6dd6" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">]</p><p name="5c78" id="5c78" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">What the above table shows is a matrix ( or an array of arrays) , which gets formatted for all the questions in the system. Now, what this vector space indicates is how each word in a sentence given in the FAQ is positioned w.r.t the entire corpus .</p><p name="c0a5" id="c0a5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A 64 bit vector is made for each sentence. It looks like an array of points distributed in a space of dimensions which are measured as the number of unique tokens of words we have.</p><p name="5fc8" id="5fc8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The technique for Word2Vec we use is the <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">skip gram modeling</strong> technique which states that given a window size “m” and a word in a sentence, we would try and predict words in the window on left and right sides</p><p name="a775" id="a775" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The model used in Noise contrastive scaling using Maximum likelihood model as shown below</p><figure name="4105" id="4105" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 455px; max-height: 111px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 24.4%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*ZPjmGl05sJUdUmRaeMP0dQ.png" data-width="455" data-height="111" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*ZPjmGl05sJUdUmRaeMP0dQ.png"></div></figure><p name="0908" id="0908" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Where score (wt,h) computes the compatibility of word wt with the context h (a dot product is commonly used). We train this model by maximizing its <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likelihood_function" data-href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likelihood_function" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">log-likelihood</a> on the training set, i.e. by maximizing</p><figure name="b24f" id="b24f" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 555px; max-height: 102px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 18.4%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*cevv-Eyn294vBgGaMHQ2EA.png" data-width="555" data-height="102" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*cevv-Eyn294vBgGaMHQ2EA.png"></div></figure><p name="6d58" id="6d58" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Once these word vectors and sentence vectors are made, it is very easy for the system to do an analogical reasoning of a question like “King is to queen as father is to ?” and the answer is a “mother” because the vectors closely match the space of mother</p><figure name="ce76" id="ce76" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 602px; max-height: 211px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 35%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*zVcfqV_MdbMhNsgAGKzq5Q.png" data-width="602" data-height="211" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*zVcfqV_MdbMhNsgAGKzq5Q.png"></div></figure><p name="6030" id="6030" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">An example picked up to demonstrate this from tensor flow tutorials is shown above. The vectors once formatted enable us to do a lot of mathematical analysis on them. For our scenario, we use these vectors to determine semantic closeness of a question asked by a user and provide answers to the user.</p><p name="1637" id="1637" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Coming back on the Entellio UI, the resultant of all of this is the formation of entities on the Entellio screen as shown below. The entity creation itself was something we refer to in the team as the “entity crisis” and it applies a hitherto unknown technique</p><figure name="649a" id="649a" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 601px; max-height: 280px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 46.6%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*CI8FJ_sC7yqdCogy0vV7Fg.png" data-width="601" data-height="280" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*CI8FJ_sC7yqdCogy0vV7Fg.png"></div></figure><p name="8aac" id="8aac" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">The above figure is a fantastic representation of a machine understanding entities and applying them to the questions put in.</p><p name="533a" id="533a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">This is the beauty of the system. While the user sees it as porting of an excel file of a give Q&amp;A, the system translates it into word vectors which can be analyzed using mathematical formulae. So now asking a question enables us to get the requisite response</p><figure name="5ab2" id="5ab2" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 602px; max-height: 286px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 47.5%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*CJVpu4KSoH4oMcaMinySJg.png" data-width="602" data-height="286" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*CJVpu4KSoH4oMcaMinySJg.png"></div></figure><p name="8f10" id="8f10" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">An example is shown in the figure above, where a question asked for not clearly intended but the systems still manages to reply back and gives the user options of other thing that he may wish to ask. The feedback buttons enable us to get enforcement from the user</p><figure name="32fc" id="32fc" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 602px; max-height: 285px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 47.3%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*VIaZqLEcpJNmmIum8yuROw.png" data-width="602" data-height="285" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*VIaZqLEcpJNmmIum8yuROw.png"></div></figure><p name="5230" id="5230" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">The above figure indicates a question answered where the bot was very sure of the answer , not showing any options to be asked beyond this.</p><p name="fe97" id="fe97" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">This is called as understanding the intentions of the user. More and more people talking to the bot system, allows this bot to retrain and learn better and get more conversant.</p><h3 name="bd07" id="bd07" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">9. REPORTING</h3><p name="7a5c" id="7a5c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">How does one determine whether answers posted by the bot are the correct answers or not? The machine in this scenario is left to act on its own and it is an arduous job for a human being to determine how it performed. While creating Entellio , we were acutely aware of this issue, so we also made the framework to produce meaningful reports to indicate the performance metric of the bot that someone makes.</p><figure name="f516" id="f516" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 602px; max-height: 288px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 47.8%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*UGSnsapog75j506Uf0lv9w.png" data-width="602" data-height="288" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*UGSnsapog75j506Uf0lv9w.png"></div></figure><figure name="2e31" id="2e31" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--figure"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 602px; max-height: 287px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 47.699999999999996%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*dTDSdoLDVi9FCcpY1S6Spw.png" data-width="602" data-height="287" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*dTDSdoLDVi9FCcpY1S6Spw.png"></div></figure><p name="ebde" id="ebde" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">The two figures above indicate the reports as seen by the administrator from within the bot framework. As a natural UI, this is how the framework can be utilised</p><ol class="postList"><li name="c07b" id="c07b" class="graf graf--li graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">Natural UI with mobile phones</strong></li></ol><figure name="96e9" id="96e9" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--li"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 540px; max-height: 219px;"><div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder-fill" style="padding-bottom: 40.6%;"></div><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*dA73nQuZYMx0GlSsDTDgHA.png" data-width="540" data-height="219" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1600/1*dA73nQuZYMx0GlSsDTDgHA.png"></div></figure><p name="c2ec" id="c2ec" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">2.</strong> <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Conversational Browsing Agent</strong></p><p name="042c" id="042c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The idea of a conversational browsing agent is to enable the same Entellio natural language UI to be a conversational browsing agent on a website. We believe that a lot of people still use corporate websites.</p><p name="675b" id="675b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">These websites have entropy associated with links in them, not allowing new or old customers to reach specific information seamlessly. Entellio can become a natural language agent on the website where it can understand queries but the web redirect mechanism described earlier can enable movement of pages on the website, a technique called conversational browsing and it is unique to Entellio as it gets packaged as part of the entire suite</p><h3 name="68ba" id="68ba" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">12. Our Belief</h3><p name="e3ed" id="e3ed" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Why do we at Maker’s lab believe in this despite the deluge of so many framework available, one might ask , and the reasons are primarily the following:</p><p name="64b7" id="64b7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">a) Connects with customers seamlessly faster: Within a couple of minutes, the system trains the word vector model and is ready to be utilised within an enterprise. All it needs is an FAQ or an excel sheet to get started</p><p name="85fa" id="85fa" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">b) Learns its way through as more and more intents gets added and more chatting happens.</p><p name="1f13" id="1f13" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">c) Does not do an NLG(natural language generation): Shouldn’t that be a con ??!!! well we do not think so. Natural language generation has found bots to become narcissistic and attain a scary personality, and since our domain was solely enterprise, we did not want this to happen. We wanted humans to contribute and tell as to what their customers would see.</p><p name="5dab" id="5dab" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">d) Departments within an enterprise can onboard new bots quicker and faster , solving myriads of L1, L2 and even L3 support issues thereby saving on OPEX(operational expenditure).</p><p name="8863" id="8863" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">e) Works seamlessly in-premise and in the cloud.</p><p name="3ae3" id="3ae3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">f) Is probably one of the few frameworks, that come with the ability of supporting transactions on the mobile, as a conversational browsing agent <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">(with ability to turn pages) </em>and an ability to send a command to any connected/IoT device.</p><p name="ccbd" id="ccbd" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">g) Self ranking of answers provided gives easy dashboards to its users.</p><p name="0bee" id="0bee" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">h) Seamlessly connects with other systems if a well define restful or a JSON based API is available.</p><p name="f5d0" id="f5d0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">i) Easily customisable and configurable</p><h3 name="6281" id="6281" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">13.
