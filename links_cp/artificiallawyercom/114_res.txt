		<h2><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12497 jetpack-lazy-image" src="https://www.artificiallawyer.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/courthouse-303370_1280-e1529671104463.png" alt width="750" height="606" data-lazy-src="https://www.artificiallawyer.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/courthouse-303370_1280-e1529671104463.png?is-pending-load=1" srcset="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7"><noscript><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12497" src="https://www.artificiallawyer.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/courthouse-303370_1280-e1529671104463.png" alt="" width="750" height="606" /></noscript></h2>
<p>Hello and welcome to the <em><strong>Artificial Lawyer Legal Tech Course Directory</strong></em>, which shows a range of educational options for those seeking to learn more about legal technology and/or to gain a recognised qualification in that area, or as part of a wider legal course subject.</p>
<p>The main listings here at present are free and the number of courses appearing will steadily grow over time. This is just the start. If you would like to include your course, whether it&#8217;s for short term study, or a full-time LLM, whether it&#8217;s in Europe, North America, Asia-Pacific or South America, Artificial Lawyer would be glad to list your legal tech education programme.</p>
<p>The courses below are listed as: short courses and post-graduate courses first, by region, then alphabetically. The following section includes law or other undergraduate courses which include a notable legal tech module, by region, then alphabetically.</p>
<p>If you are an educational establishment and would like to include your course, or would like to know more about this Directory, please drop me a line: <a href="https://www.artificiallawyer.com/contact/"><strong>Contact</strong></a>.</p>
<h3>Graduate Level and Short Courses</h3>
<h4><em><strong>North America</strong></em></h4>
<p><a href="https://www.suffolk.edu/law/">Suffolk University Law School</a></p>
<p><strong>Course title:  </strong>Legal Innovation &amp; Technology Certificate Program</p>
<p><strong>Location:</strong> This program is fully online and offered by Suffolk University Law School, which is in Boston, MA, USA</p>
<p><strong>Type of Course:  </strong>certificate program for legal professionals</p>
<p><strong>Length: </strong>6 courses that run for 10 weeks each.  The courses are offered at a rate of 2 per semester, and a student can complete the certificate in less than 1 year (or can take longer if they choose). Participation takes 2-5 hours per week, per class.<strong>    </strong></p>
<p><strong>Fee: </strong>The cost to take all six courses and obtain the certificate is $15,887.  If you do not plan to complete the certificate or are unsure if you will do so, you can simply pay for as many courses as you are interested in taking at this time. The price of the first course you take is $3,075, and each subsequent courses is discounted.</p>
<p><strong>Description of Course:</strong></p>
<p>The legal marketplace is rapidly evolving. That’s why Suffolk Law—a national leader in rethinking the delivery of legal services—is launching the online Legal Innovation &amp; Technology Certificate program.</p>
<p>This program is designed for all legal professionals – not just attorneys.  Completing the certificate will also benefit paralegals, law librarians, and others who work in the profession.</p>
<p>The program consists of six courses, which you can take individually or as part of the overall six-course certificate.</p>
<p>Enhance your legal expertise by taking online courses in:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#LegalOperations"><strong>Legal Operations</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#ProcessImprovement"><strong>Process Improvement &amp; Legal Project Management</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#21stCentury"><strong>21st Century Legal Services</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#DesignThinking"><strong>Design Thinking for Legal Professionals</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#DeliveringLegalServices"><strong>The Business of Delivering Legal Services</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.legaltechcertificate.com/courses#LegalTechnologyToolkit"><strong>Legal Technology Toolkit</strong></a></li>
</ul>
<p>Contact: <a href="http://www.legaltechcertificate.com/">www.legaltechcertificate.com</a>; the contact is the program’s faculty director: <strong>Prof. Gabe Teninbaum</strong> (gteninbaum@suffolk.edu)</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong><a href="https://law.vanderbilt.edu/">Vanderbilt Law School</a></strong></p>
<p><strong>Course title:</strong> <a href="https://law.vanderbilt.edu/courses/324">Legal Project Management</a></p>
<p><strong>Location:</strong> Nashville, Tennessee USA</p>
<p><strong>Type of Course:</strong> Law school academic offering in the J.D. program (and an Exec Ed offering in a non-academic workshop setting )</p>
<p><strong>Length:</strong> The academic offering is a semester long program of 13 weeks meeting 2 hours each week (the Exec Ed offering is a 3 day workshop)</p>
<p><strong>Fee:</strong> Both courses are priced at $3000 per student</p>
<p><strong>Description:</strong> The course examines the economic and technological state of the legal industry today. As Artificial Intelligence, blockchain technology and cryptocurrency continues to emerge, the traditional legal service delivery model is no longer adequate to meet heightened client expectations. Lean and Agile methodologies begin with a mindset change for the legal professional. The first segment of the course teaches the basics of legal project management performed manually in Excel and related static technologies. The second segment introduces the technology applications that automate legal project management and enable legal services to be delivered better, faster and cheaper with greater profitability for outside counsel and price certainty for clients.</p>
<p>Both courses are certified to qualify the students (law students and Executive Education students) for the International Institute for Legal Project Management LPP and LPA certification. See: <a href="http://www.iilpm.com/">http://www.iilpm.com/</a></p>
<p><strong>Contact:</strong> Larry.bridgesmith@law.vanderbilt.edu</p>
<p><span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;"><iframe class='youtube-player' type='text/html' width='640' height='360' src='https://www.youtube.com/embed/vJSV6RMII1U?version=3&#038;rel=1&#038;fs=1&#038;autohide=2&#038;showsearch=0&#038;showinfo=1&#038;iv_load_policy=1&#038;wmode=transparent' allowfullscreen='true' style='border:0;'></iframe></span></p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>David R. Cheriton School of Computer Science at the <a href="https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/current-graduate-students/courses/current-course-offerings/fall-2018-course-offerings">University of Waterloo</a></strong> and <strong>Osgoode Hall Law School at <a href="https://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/courses-and-seminars/legal-values-artificial-intelligence/">York University</a></strong></p>
<p><strong>Course title:</strong>  Advanced Research Topics – Artificial Intelligence: Law, Ethics, and Policy offered conjointly with Legal Values:  Artificial Intelligence</p>
<p><strong>Location: </strong> David R. Cheriton School of Computer Science at the University of Waterloo and Osgoode Hall Law School at York University, respectively (both in Ontario, <strong>Canada</strong>)</p>
<p><strong>Type of Course: </strong>Joint graduate computer science and upper-class law school seminar</p>
<p><strong>Length:</strong>  Full semester-length course</p>
<p><strong>Description of Course:</strong></p>
<p>This unique, multidisciplinary, cross-listed seminar brings together (through linked videoconference rooms at both locations) graduate students from the School of Computer Science at the University of Waterloo, and law students from Osgoode Hall Law School at York University, to explore cutting-edge legal, ethical, policy, and technical challenges implicated by the rise of artificial intelligence (“AI”), robotics, automation, and big data, in applications as diverse as autonomous vehicles, predictive policing and criminal law, sex robots, and lethal autonomous weapons.</p>
<p>Students in the course (i) learn about what is technically feasible today—and will likely be possible tomorrow; (ii) identify significant challenges that individuals and society are likely to face as a result of emerging technologies; and most importantly, (iii) explore and grapple with how the legal system and public policy is responding—or should respond in the future—to the issues presented by AI, robots, automation, and big data.</p>

Course Links:</p>

<p><strong>Location:</strong> University of Helsinki School of Law, Finland</p>
<p><strong>Type of course:</strong> Elective Bachelor’s and Master’s degree course</p>
<p><strong>Length:</strong> 5 credits (135 hours of effort)</p>
<p><strong>Fee:</strong> N/A</p>
<p><strong>Description:</strong> Social interactions, trade and services are moving to the Internet. Online sales cause novel conflicts, which are increasingly resolved through algorithmic dispute resolution platforms. At the same time the courts are updated to answer the challenges of digitalisation. Society turns to technology in order to find new ways for dispute resolution, but what does this mean in practice?</p>
<p>The course will inspect the interactions between dispute resolution and technology by evaluating, primarily, the digitalisation of legal proceedings and online dispute resolution. The main questions revolve around digitalisation, privacy, due process and the wider legal implications of information technology.</p>

<p><strong>Description:</strong> Anyone with a background in legal studies knows how alien regulations, sections and directives look to the layperson. Everyone has agreed to terms and conditions without reading them, signed contracts riddled with legalese and gotten spooked by official letters full of government jargon. The complexity of legal writing alienates the layperson from the legal system, and in the worst case scenario erodes the trust in the system itself.</p>
<p>During the course students will grasp the basics of design thinking in an interdisciplinary setting. The course will introduce the students in the role of legal design in producing clear and understandable legal writing in the future.</p>

<p><strong>Course/Module Title:</strong> &#8216;LegalTech and Access to Justice&#8217; &#8211; Part of Undergraduate Course, for final year law students.</p>
<p><strong>Location: </strong>The University of Manchester School of Law, Manchester, UK</p>
<p><strong>Length:</strong> 12 weeks.</p>
<p><strong>Fee:</strong> N/A.</p>
<p><strong>Description:</strong> The emergence of LegalTech is one of the most significant trends in the legal services sector for decades. This project-based course aims to provide an academic and practical introduction to LegalTech. Student teams are assigned to non-profit clients, and work together under supervision on a project to build an app to solve a real-world ‘access to justice’ problem identified by their client.</p>
<p>The apps are built using the <a href="https://www.neotalogic.com/">Neota Logic</a> software development platform. Weekly seminars will explore the access to justice crisis, how digital tools can help to address it, and how to design these tools using the Neota platform. The seminar classes are supported by weekly homework assignments.</p>
<p>The course will end with a competition in which student teams present their apps to a panel of judges. The winners are selected based on criteria including effectiveness and creativity. After the course, the apps are deployed to leverage the students&#8217; work to address real-world legal and/or justice challenges over and over again on behalf of the non-profit clients.</p>
