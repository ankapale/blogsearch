Its continued development, guided by the following principles, will offer amazing opportunities to help and empower people in the decades and centuries ahead.</p></div></div><div class="sqs-block code-block sqs-block-code" data-block-type="23" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1509583797994_34098"><div class="sqs-block-content"><script async src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"></script>

</script></div></div><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1509583797994_34165"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h2>Research Issues</h2></div></div><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-1 span-1"><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_85564"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-11 span-11"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_84739"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h3>Research Goal</h3><p>The goal of AI research should be to create not undirected intelligence, but beneficial intelligence.</p><h3>Research Funding</h3><p>Investments in AI should be accompanied by funding for research on ensuring its beneficial use, including thorny questions in computer science, economics, law, ethics, and social studies, such as:</p><ul><li>How can we make future AI systems highly robust, so that they do what we want without malfunctioning or getting hacked?</li><li>How can we grow our prosperity through automation while maintaining people‚Äôs resources and purpose?</li><li>How can we update our legal systems to be more fair and efficient, to keep pace with AI, and to manage the risks associated with AI?</li><li>What set of values should AI be aligned with, and what legal and ethical status should it have?</li></ul><h3>Science-Policy Link</h3><p>There should be constructive and healthy exchange between AI researchers and policy-makers.</p><h3>Research Culture</h3><p>A culture of cooperation, trust, and transparency should be fostered among researchers and developers of AI.</p><h3>Race Avoidance</h3><p>Teams developing AI systems should actively cooperate to avoid corner-cutting on safety standards.</p></div></div></div></div><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1487001650248_38430"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h2>Ethics and Values</h2></div></div><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-1 span-1"><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_91885"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-11 span-11"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_90733"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h3>Safety</h3><p>AI systems should be safe and secure throughout their operational lifetime, and verifiably so where applicable and feasible.</p><h3>Failure Transparency</h3><p>If an AI system causes harm, it should be possible to ascertain why.</p><h3>Judicial Transparency</h3><p>Any involvement by an autonomous system in judicial decision-making should provide a satisfactory explanation auditable by a competent human authority.</p><h3>Responsibility</h3><p>Designers and builders of advanced AI systems are stakeholders in the moral implications of their use, misuse, and actions, with a responsibility and opportunity to shape those implications.</p><h3>Value Alignment</h3><p>Highly autonomous AI systems should be designed so that their goals and behaviors can be assured to align with human values throughout their operation.</p><h3>Human Values</h3><p>AI systems should be designed and operated so as to be compatible with ideals of human dignity, rights, freedoms, and cultural diversity.</p><h3>Personal Privacy</h3><p>People should have the right to access, manage and control the data they generate, given AI systems‚Äô power to analyze and utilize that data.</p><h3>Liberty and Privacy</h3><p>The application of AI to personal data must not unreasonably curtail people‚Äôs real or perceived liberty.</p><h3>Shared Benefit</h3><p>AI technologies should benefit and empower as many people as possible.</p><h3>Shared Prosperity</h3><p>The economic prosperity created by AI should be shared broadly, to benefit all of humanity.</p><h3>Human Control</h3><p>Humans should choose how and whether to delegate decisions to AI systems, to accomplish human-chosen objectives.</p><h3>Non-subversion</h3><p>The power conferred by control of highly advanced AI systems should respect and improve, rather than subvert, the social and civic processes on which the health of society depends.</p><h3>AI Arms Race</h3><p>An arms race in lethal autonomous weapons should be avoided.</p></div></div></div></div><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1487001650248_37160"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h2>Longer-term Issues</h2></div></div><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-1 span-1"><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_98316"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-11 span-11"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1502396707943_97023"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h3>Capability Caution</h3><p>There being no consensus, we should avoid strong assumptions regarding upper limits on future AI capabilities.</p><h3>Importance</h3><p>Advanced AI could represent a profound change in the history of life on Earth, and should be planned for and managed with commensurate care and resources.</p><h3>Risks</h3><p>Risks posed by AI systems, especially catastrophic or existential risks, must be subject to planning and mitigation efforts commensurate with their expected impact.</p><h3>Recursive Self-Improvement</h3><p>AI systems designed to recursively self-improve or self-replicate in a manner that could lead to rapidly increasing quality or quantity must be subject to strict safety and control measures.</p><h3>Common Good</h3><p>Superintelligence should only be developed in the service of widely shared ethical ideals, and for the benefit of all humanity rather than one state or organization.</p></div></div></div></div><div class="sqs-block button-block sqs-block-button"
